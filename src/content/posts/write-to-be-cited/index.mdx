SGEO (Generative Engine Optimization) optimizes for AI systems that synthesize a single trusted answer. If ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google AI Overview is where your audience finds things now, the question is not whether your page ranks. It is whether your page gets cited.

The mechanics are different. Keyword stuffing does nothing here. AI search pulls from multiple sources, compresses them, and surfaces the most trustworthy, coherent signal. Your page competes to be that signal.
## What the data shows

[Research on GEO published in 2024](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.09735) tested which content tactics improved visibility in AI generated answers. The top performers were not technical tricks. They were writing fundamentals like adding credible citations, including specific statistics, incorporating relevant quotes, and clear/concise writing. Those tactics boosted AI visibility by up to 40 percent. The bottom performers were keyword repetition and unnecessary descriptors which is exactly what traditional SEO rewarded.

## A simple GEO habit

Before publishing, make sure your post includes at least one clear, source backed claim.

If everything on the page is vague, an AI system has nothing useful to cite. “Our API is powerful” does not say much. “Our API processes 10,000 requests per second with p99 latency under 50ms, independently verified by Datadog” gives the reader a specific claim, a number, and a source.

That habit surfaces four questions worth answering on every page:

- What does this do, specifically?
- Who is this for, and why does it matter to them?
- What number or outcome proves the claim?
- What external source backs it up?

## The underlying principle

This is not a new algorithm to game. It is what good writing has always required. Specificity and clear communication earns trust -> trust earns citations -> citations earn visibility.
